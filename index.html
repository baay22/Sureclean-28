What You Need To Know About Mattress Cleaning

✲ Mattresses are a breeding ground for dust mites and bacteria

Did you know that your mattress is home to millions of dust mites? These tiny creatures live in the fibers of your mattress and feed on dead skin cells. They often cause asthma and allergies. Bacteria and other microorganisms on your bedding can cause odors and health issues.

✲ More than changing your sheets is required.

While changing your sheets regularly is essential, more is needed to keep your mattress clean. Dust mites, bacteria, and other contaminants can still accumulate in the fibers of your mattress. That’s why it’s essential to clean your mattress regularly.

✲ Vacuuming is not enough.

Vacuuming your mattress is a good start, but it won’t remove all the dust mites, bacteria, and other contaminants deeply embedded in the fibers. A professional mattress cleaning service is needed to clean your mattress thoroughly.

✲ Professional cleaning can improve your health

Professional cleaning can reduce allergies and asthma by removing dust mites, bacteria, and other contaminants.

✲ Cleaning your mattress can extend its life

Cleaning your mattress regularly removes dirt and grit that can break down the fibers.

✲ DIY cleaning can be risky

While many DIY cleaning methods are available, they can be risky. Using the wrong cleaning products or techniques can damage your mattress and void the warranty.

Professional mattress cleaning services use specialized equipment and safe cleaning products that won’t damage your mattress or harm your health. Tips on How to Clean a Mattress

✓ Vacuum the mattress

Use a vacuum cleaner with a crevice tool to thoroughly clean your mattress’s surface. This will help remove any loose dirt and debris.

✓ Spot clean stains

For stubborn stains, you can use a mixture of water and mild detergent to spot clean. Use a clean towel to dry the cushion after using as little water as possible.

✓ Deodorize the mattress

Sprinkle baking soda across the mattress and let it set for several hours. (or overnight, if possible). Then, vacuum up the baking soda using the crevice tool.

✓ Rotate the mattress

Every few months, rotate your mattress to ensure even wear and tear. This prevents dust mites and other pathogens from gathering.

✓ Use a mattress protector

For spills, stains, and allergens, use a mattress cover.

These tips can help keep your mattress clean, but an expert mattress cleaning service is the best way to remove dust mites, bacteria, and other contaminants from deep within the fibers.

Factors that Affect the Mattress Cleaning Price

The price of professional mattress cleaning can vary depending on several factors, including:

✦ Size of the mattress

The larger the mattress, the more time and cleaning products will be required. This means more oversized beds will generally cost more to clean than smaller ones.

✦ Type of mattress

Different mattresses may require different cleaning methods and products, affecting the price. For example, a memory foam mattress may require a different cleaning approach than a traditional innerspring mattress.

✦ The severity of stains

If your mattress has significant stains or odor issues, cleaning can require more time and effort, which can increase the price.

✦ Additional services

Mattress cleaning services that give upholstery or stain protection may charge more.

✦ Location

The mattress cleaning cost may also vary depending on your site. Urban areas with a higher cost of living may charge more. It’s essential to prioritize quality and effectiveness over price when choosing a mattress cleaner.

A reputable and experienced mattress cleaning service may charge more, but they will use safe and effective cleaning products and methods to ensure your mattress is thoroughly cleaned and sanitized.
